Core Strategy

Why start internal? Sustainable strategy isn't built on "shoulds" or external pressure. It must be rooted in your authentic core. Before we map out tactics, deadlines, and weekly schedules, we must excavate the foundation. This section guides you from your core identity to a concrete, actionable vision for the future.

1.2. The Intersection

Where do your Passions (what you love), your Strengths (what you are excellent at), and Usefulness (what the world needs) overlap?

Phase 2: Project the Future (The Destination)

Collins & Porras

The 10-Year BHAG (Big Hairy Audacious Goal)

Define a goal that is 50% terrifying and 50% exhilarating. It should be a mountain you aren’t sure you can climb, but you know you want to climb.

Phase 3: Articulate the Vision (The Draft)

Heath Brothers: Made to Stick

A vision must be clear and sticky. Use the "Be-Do-Have" method to structure your statement.

The Formula:

"To be [Character/Identity] who [Actions/Contribution] so that [Ultimate Impact/Legacy]."

Drafting Prompts:

  • Internal: "I envision a life where I am..."
  • External: "I envision a world where..."
  • The Bridge: "My role in creating this is..."

Example:

"To be a fearless compassionate leader (Identity) who empowers marginalized voices through storytelling (Action) so that every child sees themselves represented in media (Impact)."

Phase 4: The Stress Test (Validation)

Greg McKeown: Essentialism

Read your draft above aloud. Does it pass these three checks?

Implementation: Strategy to Reality

Part 6: The Logic Check

Strategy Validation

Stress-test your strategy. Ensure your inputs (resources) across all topics are realistic and directly lead to your desired Impact.

The Critical Question

"If you consistently execute your 'Must Have' activities, are you confident you will achieve the goals defined above?"

Part 7: The Weekly Operating System

Cal Newport: Time Blocking

Anchor your "Must-Have" habits in your weekly planner. If it gets scheduled, it gets done. If you cannot find a time slot, you must face a hard truth: either the activity changes, or your other priorities do. Make it fit, or make it go.
