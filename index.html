Core Strategy

Why start internally? Sustainable strategy isn't built on "shoulds" or external pressure. It must be rooted in your authentic core. Before mapping tactics, deadlines, and schedules, we need to excavate the foundation. This section guides you from your core identity to a concrete, actionable vision for the future.

1.2. The Intersection

Where do your passions (what you love), strengths (what you excel at), and usefulness (what the world needs) overlap?

Phase 2: Project the Future (The Destination)

Collins & Porras

The 10-Year BHAG (Big Hairy Audacious Goal)

Define a goal that's 50% terrifying and 50% exhilarating. It should be a mountain you aren't sure you can climb, but know you want to.

Phase 3: Articulate the Vision (The Draft)

Heath Brothers: Made to Stick

A vision must be clear and sticky. Use the "Be-Do-Have" method to structure your statement.

The Formula:

"To be [Character/Identity] who [Actions/Contribution] so that [Ultimate Impact/Legacy]."

Drafting Prompts:

  • Internal: "I envision a life where I am..."
  • External: "I envision a world where..."
  • The Bridge: "My role in creating this is..."

Example:

"To be a fearless compassionate leader (Identity) who empowers marginalized voices through storytelling (Action) so that every child sees themselves represented in media (Impact)."

Phase 4: The Stress Test (Validation)

Greg McKeown: Essentialism

Read your draft aloud. Does it pass these three checks?

First: A Short Intro on Dopamine

Why do we need it to succeed?

Dopamine isn't just about pleasure—it's the molecule of more. It is the universal currency of foraging and seeking. To succeed in your goals, you must learn to spike dopamine during the effort, not just at the finish line.
